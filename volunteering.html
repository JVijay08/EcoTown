Volunteering

Explore volunteering opportunities in our area and state!

Bring One for the Chipper

Help us the Keep Georgia Beautiful Foundation and local partners recycle Christmas trees into mulch. Volunteers are needed at drop-off sites (such as Home Depot locations) to help direct traffic and move trees!

Estimated Time: As long as you can commit

MLK Day of Service: River Cleanup

Join Chatahoochee Riverkeeper for a high-impact cleanup to honor Dr. King's legacy. This is a get-dirty even where you'll help remove litter from the river's tributaries.

Estimated Time: 3 hours

MLK Day of Service: Park Beautification

Panola Mountain State Park is hosting a volunteer day dedicated to park maintenance and preservation projects to honor Dr.King's legacy.

Estimated Time: 2 hours

Plant Care in the BeltLine Arboretum

Work with Trees Atlanta on the Eastside Trail to care for the urban forest through mulching, weeding, and invasive species removal.

Estimated Time: 3 hours

Downtown Tree Care

Help Trees Atlanta maintain street and park trees in the heart of downtown to ensure the urban canopy thrives.

Estimated Time: 3 hours

Second Saturday Workday

The Dunwoody Nature Center hosts monthly community workdays focusing on trail maintenance and removing invasive plants like English Ivy.

Estimated Time: 3 hours

Docent Training: Introduction & Overview

If you want a long-term role, Trees Atlanta begins its 2026 Docent Training. You’ll learn to lead tours and educate the public about Georgia’s native trees.

Estimated Time: However long you want to commit

River Cleanup with Trust for Public Land

A partnership cleanup between Chattahoochee Riverkeeper and the Trust for Public Land at a location to be determined based on where trash has accumulated.

Estimated Time: 3 hours

Georgia Wildlife Resources Division

The WRD looks for volunteers to help with wildlife stewardship, including maintaining access to wild places and assisting with conservation education events.

Estimated Time: However long you want to commit
